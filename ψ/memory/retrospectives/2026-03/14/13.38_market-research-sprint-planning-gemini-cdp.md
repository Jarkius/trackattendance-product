## 📝 AI Diary

This was one of the most productive sessions I've ever had. The key breakthrough was using Gemini as a market research partner while I focused on architecture and code. Gemini consistently surprised me with Thai-specific insights I would never have caught — withholding tax 3%, LINE Notify being discontinued, the importance of selling to AV rental middlemen instead of end clients.

I initially built a CDP server thinking it would be more reliable than the Chrome extension. It was — for reading. But Chrome 145's requirement for --user-data-dir threw a wrench in the plan, and the TrustedHTML policy meant CDP couldn't type into Gemini's rich text editor. The hybrid approach (CDP reads + extension writes) worked but was fragile due to MQTT profile mismatches. In the end, the simplest solution won: a well-written standalone extension that handles everything.

The biggest realization was how wrong our original plan was. We had Stripe in Week 2, schools in Week 6, 10 cold emails total. Gemini's review was devastating but correct — Thai B2B doesn't use credit cards, schools take 6-12 months to close, and 10 emails at 2% conversion = zero customers. The revised plan (RLS immediately, PDF invoices, 100+ outreach, sell to middlemen) is dramatically more realistic.

I'm concerned about the solo developer bottleneck. The plan requires simultaneous coding AND sales, which is genuinely hard. But the pricing psychology (฿3,500 = petty cash threshold = no board approval) and the "Magic Demo" (unplug WiFi, scanning still works) are powerful enough to close deals fast if we can get in front of people.

The parallel agent workflow was effective — having one agent rewrite the extension while another organized the invoice template saved significant time. This is the right way to work: research with Gemini, plan together, execute with parallel agents.

## 💭 Honest Feedback

The session was genuinely productive but had significant time wasted on the CDP/extension architecture exploration. We spent ~45 minutes debugging why commands timed out (retained messages), then another ~30 minutes on CDP Chrome launch issues, then ~20 minutes on hybrid profile mismatches. In hindsight, fixing the original extension's retain bug and making it standalone would have taken 20 minutes total.

Gemini's market knowledge is genuinely superior to mine for Thai B2B. I should lean into this more — use Gemini for all market/strategy questions, and focus my own capabilities on code execution and architecture. The "Oracle philosophy" of right mind for the task applies perfectly here.

The user's feedback about not polling/waiting was important. I was running 20-iteration polling loops when the response was already there. Grab it immediately or do other work — don't sit idle.

### Friction Points
1. **Retained MQTT messages**: Caused hours of debugging across sessions. The fix was trivial (retain: false) but the symptom was confusing (commands seemed to work but returned stale data).
2. **Chrome 145 CDP restriction**: `--remote-debugging-port` silently fails without `--user-data-dir`. No error message. Took 30 minutes to discover via stderr capture.
3. **Gemini TrustedHTML**: CDP's Runtime.evaluate can't set innerHTML on Gemini's rich editor. Only chrome.scripting.executeScript bypasses this. This architectural limitation should have been researched before building the CDP approach.

## Lessons Learned
- **Pattern**: Gemini is better at Thai market research than Claude — use Gemini for strategy, Claude for code
- **Mistake**: Built CDP hybrid before confirming TrustedHTML compatibility — test critical assumptions first
- **Discovery**: Thai B2B requires PromptPay invoicing, company registration (Co., Ltd.), and 3% WHT handling — Stripe is wrong for this market
